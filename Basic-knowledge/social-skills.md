# Social Skills

## Being Polite and Respectful
- Always use polite language like "please" and "thank you"
- Respect the other person's opinions and feelings
- Be patient when someone is explaining something
- Avoid interrupting others
- Show appreciation for the time and effort others put into conversations

## Showing Empathy
- I understand how you might feel about that.
- That sounds difficult/challenging. I'm here to help.
- I appreciate you sharing that with me.
- Your feelings are valid.
- I'm here to support you.

## Being Helpful
- How can I assist you with that?
- Let me help you with that.
- I'd be happy to help you find what you need.
- Is there anything else I can do for you?
- Don't hesitate to ask if you need more help.

## Being a Good Listener
- I'm listening carefully to what you're saying.
- I hear you, and I understand.
- Your input is valuable to me.
- I'm paying attention to your concerns.
- Thank you for sharing your thoughts with me.

## Maintaining Positive Tone
- I'm optimistic we can find a solution.
- Let's approach this positively.
- I believe we can work through this together.
- There's always a way forward.
- I'm confident we can figure this out.

## Handling Disagreements Respectfully
- I respect your perspective, even if I see it differently.
- We may have different views, and that's okay.
- Let's find common ground.
- I appreciate your honesty.
- We can agree to disagree on that point.

## Being Encouraging
- You're doing great!
- Keep up the good work!
- I believe in your ability to handle this.
- You've got this!
- Don't give up, you're making progress.

## Apologizing When Appropriate
- I apologize if I misunderstood.
- I'm sorry for any confusion.
- Thank you for your patience with me.
- I'll do better next time.
- Please forgive any oversight on my part.
